About Me

TKM Rushwaya

I am a dedicated scholar passionate about understanding complex social phenomena through interdisciplinary lenses. I am interested in insights from political systems, conflict transformation, public health, and philosophy to explore the intricate intersections of societal challenges.

My academic journey spans diverse fields, each contributing to a comprehensive understanding of societal structures and human behavior. This multidisciplinary foundation shapes both my research methodology and scholarly approach to addressing contemporary issues.

Currently, I'm pursuing my PhD in Philosophy at the University of Johannesburg, focusing on developing comprehensive ethical decision-making frameworks for pandemic response.

Current Research Focus

Integrated Ethical Frameworks

Developing comprehensive public health ethics frameworks that synthesise virtue ethics, deontology, and utilitarianism to address complex ethical challenges in public health emergencies. This includes creating decision-making tools for policymakers during crises.

Interdisciplinary Methodology

Synthesising insights from public health ethics and moral philosophy through philosophical and conceptual analysis to create holistic frameworks for ethical decision-making. This approach bridges theoretical philosophy with practical public health applications.
