About Me

I am a dedicated scholar passionate about understanding complex social phenomena through interdisciplinary lenses. I am interested in insights from political systems, conflict transformation, public health, and philosophy to explore the intricate intersections of societal challenges.

My academic journey spans diverse fields, each contributing to a comprehensive understanding of societal structures and human behavior. This multidisciplinary foundation shapes both my research methodology and scholarly approach to addressing contemporary issues.

Research Focus

Political Participation & Moral Dilemmas

My past research has extensively explored political participation, dissent mechanisms, and complex moral dilemmas within various socio-political contexts, examining how these elements interact and influence decision-making processes.

Current Focus: Decision-Making Frameworks

My current doctoral research focuses on developing integrated decision-making frameworks for pandemic response. I am particularly interested in creating a comprehensive public health ethics framework that synthesises elements of virtue ethics, deontology, and utilitarianism to address complex ethical challenges in public health emergencies.

Methodological Approach

Integrating applied ethics with rigorous philosophical analysis to develop practical frameworks for real-world ethical challenges.

Interdisciplinary Perspective

Synthesising insights from political science, public health ethics, conflict studies, and moral philosophy to create holistic analytical frameworks.
