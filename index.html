Current Research Focus

My current research integrates insights from moral philosophy and public health ethics to create a comprehensive framework for ethical decision-making in pandemic response.

Framework

Towards an Integrated Decision-Making Framework

  • • Synthesising virtue ethics, deontology, and utilitarianism into a coherent model.
  • • Developing guidance for ethical decision-making during pandemics.
  • • Creating adaptable frameworks for complex public health scenarios.
Three Pillars of Ethical Decision-Making
Virtue Ethics
  • • Grounded in Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics.
  • • Focusing on the doctrine of mean.
  • • Examining its role in pandemic decision-making.
Deontology
  • • Analysing duties and obligations.
  • • Examining moral rightness regardless of outcomes.
  • • Applying categorical imperatives in public health.
≥
Utilitarianism
  • • Examining actions that produce greatest good
  • • Focusing on consequentialism
  • • Evaluating whether minimisng harm yields morally sound decision outcomes
Approach

Interdisciplinary Methodology

  • • Using philosophical analysis with public health ethics to develop holistic frameworks.
  • • Creating frameworks that are both theoretically rigorous, and practically applicable.
Philosophical Analysis
  • • Applying critical reasoning to moral foundations.
  • • Examining moral foundations for decisions/decision-making.
  • • Exploring ethical theories that guide responsible action.
Conceptual Analysis
  • • Clarifying complex ideas and defining key concepts
  • • Ensuring precise communication in ethical discourse
  • • Establishing rigorous theoretical grounding
Public Health Ethics
  • • Integrating ethical considerations for public health
  • • Focusing on population well-being and justice
  • • Balancing individual rights and collective safety
